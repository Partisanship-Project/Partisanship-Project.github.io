What is Partisanship?

A Brief Overview

  1. What is partisanship - partisanship has traditionally meant favoring one's side. Yet in a democracy, there are many sides to any one debate. These sides get synthesized and simplified by political parties, ideologies, and social movements. 
  2. Historical research on partisanship - The modern study of partisanship starts with the study of political parties. Researchers have wanted to know the extent to which political parties determined how individual politicians would act. The stronger the party's constraint over its members, the stronger their partisanship.
  3. Recent trends in partisanship - Recent work is showing that political parties are becoming more ideologically aligned. Democrats have more liberal ideologies and republicans express more conservative ideology. Whereas before parties could constrain members by giving or withholding rewards, now ideology is the lingua franca of party power.

Why the Partisanship Project

Advancing the study of partisanship 

We believe the amount of data being generated by and about politics should be put to its fullest use to answer the most important questions of our time. The Partisanship Project utilizes big data to understand how partisanship is changing in real time.
